How it works

The selection process

Identification

Players are identified through training sessions, program participation, and coach observation. We look for technical ability, competitive mentality, and coachability as foundational qualities.

Evaluation

Selected players are assessed in a training context — not a formal tryout. We evaluate how they move, how they make decisions, how they respond to pressure, and how they carry themselves under demand.

Development

Players who join Selectivo enter a structured development environment. Standards are maintained, progress is tracked, and every player is challenged to keep rising. The roster is earned, and kept, through continued commitment.

Elite by standard Not everyone qualifies.
That is the point.

Selectivo is built on the principle that a selective environment produces better results. When every player around you has been chosen, the room raises itself.

Selection criteria

What we look for.

Technical quality that stands out in competitive situations. Coachability — the genuine willingness to hear feedback and apply it immediately. A competitive mindset that competes with purpose, not just effort.

Players who show discipline, focus, and the ambition to keep improving even when training is difficult. These are not qualities you coach into someone who does not already carry them.

If your child has these qualities, the evaluation process will reflect that.

For ambitious families

Why this matters for your child

Why not a regular program?

In a standard environment, your child competes at the average of that group. In Selectivo, they train at the top of ours. The ceiling is higher. The development is faster. The standards are real. Average training produces average results — regardless of how talented the individual is.

Why does selection matter?

Selective environments produce better players because better players surround them. Selection is not exclusion — it is an investment in the quality of the environment your child develops inside. The room shapes the player as much as the coaching does.

What will my child gain?

Technical improvement under real coaching pressure. Competitive confidence from consistently training at a higher level. Professional habits that transfer beyond football. And a genuine pathway into the best environments the sport offers — built from here.
